Design and Progress

I modeled my design after the archimedes spiral which is known to be one of the most efficient turbine blades.
When first Designing the blade in Onshape, it took about 2 classes to come up with the blade I wanted and to fully render it in the software.
After I decided the design was ready for the first print I emailed the file to Max and he was able to print the first trial blade at home.
Laying out this project beforehand in stages was a helpful way to stay on track and make sure we were able to
collect data in the wind tunnel.

Trial and error

When Max brought the blade back into class I realized some mistakes that I had made. The first mistake was
that there was no set screw hole for the blade to be attached to the shaft through. Another flaw was that
the balde was smaller and thinner than I hoped. And lastly this first print had the outter blades extruded through
the middle shaft hole, this made it impossible for it to be put on the base. Through these flaws I designed an upgraded version
of my blade and corrected them all. I printed my next one on a creator pro 3d printer in the basement of the school.
